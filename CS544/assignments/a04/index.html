Question 1

In order to show a continuous range of values, a sequential color map utilizes a variety of colors in a certain order. Typically, data with a range of low to high values are represented using this style of color map.
A diverging color map, on the other hand, combines two contrasting colors to indicate values that fluctuate around a central point or a reference value. This kind of color map is frequently used to depict data that has a natural midpoint or reference point, such as data that has a natural zero point or data that spans from positive to negative values.
I would choose a sequential color scheme since it can clearly show the relative size differences between the categories or subcategories that are being shown. Also, the use of a consistent color scheme makes it easy to compare the sizes of different categories or subcategories, as well as to identify the largest and smallest categories or subcategories.

Scenario where treemaps are a better choice:
Suppose We are examining a sizable amount of hierarchically organized data, similar to a directory of files on your computer. The size of each file and folder in the directory, as well as how they are nested inside one another, should be visible. A treemap might be a good option in this situation. A file or folder would be represented by a rectangle in the treemap, and the rectangle's size would correspond to the file or folder's size. To demonstrate the hierarchical links between the files and directories, the rectangles would be nested inside of one another. As a result, it would be simple for us to determine which files and folders are occupying the most space as well as how they are arranged inside the directory.

Scenario where node-link diagrams are a better choice:
Suppose we are analyzing a social network and want to see how the participants are connected to one another. A node-link diagram would be the best option in this scenario. The diagram's nodes stand in for individual users, while the connections between them signify their connections. The user clusters that are more tightly related to one another might be highlighted in the diagram by how it is established. Using this, we may quickly determine the network's organizational structure, the users that are more important or influential, and the communities that are present.
